How We Calculate This

Our model is grounded in the same industry-standard tools that professional solar installers use, adapted for the unique realities of NYC balcony installations.

NREL PVWatts V8 Engine

The core energy model is NREL's PVWatts, maintained by the US Department of Energy. It uses the 2020 National Solar Radiation Database with 8,760 hourly weather data points for a typical meteorological year at NYC's coordinates.

Azimuth-Based Production

We model production for all 8 compass directions using PVWatts reference data for NYC (40.75°N). South-facing produces ~100% of baseline, SE/SW ~92%, E/W ~72%, NE/NW ~45%, North ~32%. These factors account for NYC's latitude and solar geometry.

Vertical Tilt Adjustment

Balcony panels at 90° (vertical railing mount) produce ~70-85% of optimal tilt output. PVWatts handles this natively. Angled mounts at 70° recover ~12-15% more energy. We use tilt=90 or tilt=70 depending on your selection.

Shadow Derating Model

NYC's urban canyon effect is the biggest variable. We use a simplified derating model based on floor level, building height, and neighborhood density. Factors range from 0.60 (low floor, dense) to 0.95 (penthouse, clear). We also apply a 1.03 thermal bonus for better balcony airflow.

Financial Model

Savings use Con Edison SC-1 all-in residential rate of $0.22/kWh. Payback accounts for 3% annual electricity rate escalation. 25-year lifetime value includes both rate escalation and 0.5% annual panel degradation. CO₂ uses NYC grid factor of 0.65 lbs/kWh.

Monthly Distribution

Monthly production follows NYC's seasonal solar resource pattern from NREL data. Peak production in June-July (~12% of annual), minimum in December (~5%). This seasonal curve is adjusted by your specific orientation and shade profile.
