Frequently asked questions

Is this legal in NYC?+

Not yet. The SUNNY Act (S8512/A9111) is currently moving through the New York State Legislature. Once passed, it will legalize plug-in solar panels up to 800W for residential use.

Do I need my landlord's permission?+

No. Once the SUNNY Act passes, tenants will have the right to install plug-in solar without landlord approval — similar to how window AC units work today.

Can I take it with me when I move?+

Yes. Plug-in solar panels are portable by design — they mount to your railing and plug into a standard outlet. When you move, we'll reinstall at your new place.

How much will I save?+

Based on the SUNNY Act's fiscal analysis, NYC renters can expect to save approximately $279 per year. With NYC's electricity rate at 31¢/kWh — 56% above the national average — balcony solar pays for itself fast.
